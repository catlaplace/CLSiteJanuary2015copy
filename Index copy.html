Development of insulitis in susceptible mice


Illustration for a book chapter.
Autoimmune Diabetes. Diane Mathis and Jason Gaglia. Molecular Imaging, by Ralph Weissleder, Brian D Ross, Alnanawaz Rehemtull, Sanjiv Sam Gambhir. Pmph Usa; 1 st edition. April 30, 2009.



In mice of all strains, a ripple of β-cell death occurs physiologically approximately 2 weeks of age. In susceptible mice, antigens derived from dead β-cells (shown in black) are taken up by dendritic cells (DCs) (blue cells) in the pancreatic islets (left), inducing their migration to the pancreatic lymph node (PLN) (right) with their maturation en route. In the PLN, the DCs present β-cell-derived antigens to naive β-cell-reactive T cells (green cells) in circulation and activate them. Upon recognition of major histocompatibility complex molecule-β-cell peptide complexes, the naive T cells become activated (red cells), acquiring the ability to migrate through the tissues. In the islets, the activated T cells re-encounter cognate antigen are retained and become reactivated, thus initiating insulitis.

About Me

I combine a PhD in Biological and Medical Sciences with Graphic Design Skills.

As a postdoctoral fellow, I have developed mouse models to decipher causes and triggers of bone diseases such as osteoporosis and osteoarthritis. While doing research, I attended continuing education at the School of Museum of Fine Arts, and completed a Certificate of Graphic Design.

I then decided to leave the bench and join the research team of Dr. Benoist and Dr. Mathis, at Harvard Medical School as a digital content producer. My responsibilities involve creating strong visual representations, graphs and illustrations to support scientific publications, user interfaces and websites, aimed at both scientific and non scientific audiences.

 

Painting has been a hobby of mine for a few years. You can have a glimpse here.
